# Semantic Trajectory Planning as Sequential Information-Gain Retrieval: A Frontier-Weighted Bridge Score for Multi-Hop RAG

**An independent-research preprint (v2). Honest-positioning draft.**

---

## Abstract

Retrieval-augmented generation (RAG) has already moved decisively away from one-shot top-K nearest-neighbor lookup: iterative, active, and agentic retrieval methods reformulate retrieval as a sequential, history-conditioned, often uncertainty-driven decision process, and a substantial body of work formalizes it as a Markov decision process trained with reinforcement learning. We do **not** claim to introduce this paradigm shift; it predates this work by years. Instead, we make a narrow, defensible contribution. We define **Semantic Trajectory Planning (STP)** as a finite-horizon belief-MDP over a *discrete* corpus graph whose per-step acquisition score is the expected information gain (EIG) about the answer, and we introduce a single new functional within it: a **frontier-weighted Bridge Score** — a state-conditioned, answer-relevance-weighted betweenness over the cut between the explored set and the currently-unreachable answer frontier, with a tractable diffusion surrogate and a supervised target of *reachable-gold-evidence gain*. We show formally that this Bridge Score reduces, under specific parameter settings, to classical betweenness centrality (Freeman, 1977), to the bridging coefficient (Hwang et al., 2008), and to query-free curiosity bonuses — and that it differs from each, and from HopRAG-style answer-relevance edge scoring, precisely in being *jointly* query-conditioned and frontier-seeking. We argue from primary geometric results that the continuous-manifold framing common to "semantic navigation" proposals is not viable and adopt the discrete-graph substrate throughout. We present an experiment design whose pre-registered confirm/falsify criteria make the contribution falsifiable: STP must produce a *selective, per-cost* improvement on genuinely bridge-requiring multi-hop items against HopRAG and strong iterative-RAG baselines, localized by ablation to the frontier term, or the contribution does not stand. We are explicit throughout about which of STP's mechanisms are pre-empted (most of them) and which single component survives as incremental novelty.

## 1. Introduction

The premise that retrieval for knowledge-intensive generation should be sequential rather than one-shot, history-aware rather than memoryless, and driven by the model's information need rather than by raw similarity is now the mainstream position in the field, not a frontier claim. Active retrieval triggers retrieval on generation uncertainty [FLARE, 2023](https://arxiv.org/abs/2305.06983); interleaved retrieval conditions each query on the evolving reasoning chain [IRCoT, 2023](https://arxiv.org/abs/2212.10509); reflection-token controllers learn when to retrieve and critique what is retrieved [Self-RAG, 2024](https://arxiv.org/abs/2310.11511); complexity routers select among retrieval strategies of differing aggressiveness [Adaptive-RAG, 2024](https://arxiv.org/abs/2403.14403); and reinforcement-learned agents take multi-turn retrieval actions in a loop [Search-R1, 2025](https://arxiv.org/abs/2503.09516). The MDP framing of iterative retrieval is itself published and evaluated [Stop-RAG, 2025](https://arxiv.org/abs/2510.14337). Any honest contribution in this space must therefore *start* from this consensus, not announce it.

The motivating failure case we target is narrow and specific: a multi-hop query whose gold evidence chain contains at least one hop across a **low-similarity edge**, where the next required passage is not among the nearest neighbors of the accumulated context. Such "bridge hops" are precisely what compositional multi-hop benchmarks are constructed to require [MuSiQue, 2022](https://arxiv.org/abs/2108.00573); a concrete intuition is a chain such as *build systems → dependency graphs → SAT solving → graph theory → scheduling*, where each link is conceptually adjacent but no single similarity query spans the chain. Network science has long held that exactly these long-range "bridge" ties carry novel, non-redundant information [Granovetter, 1973](https://www.jstor.org/stable/2776392).

Our contribution is one functional and the formal apparatus needed to state it precisely. We define STP as a belief-MDP whose acquisition score is EIG about the answer (Section 4), and within it a **frontier-weighted Bridge Score** (Section 4.5) that scores a candidate node by how much it expands reachability of the answer-relevant, currently-unreachable frontier from the agent's explored set. We make three things explicit and verifiable: (i) the discrete corpus graph is the only coherent substrate (Section 3); (ii) the Bridge Score's relationship to its classical and learned antecedents is a strict generalization with a named additional term (Section 4.5, Section 6); and (iii) the empirical bar for the claim to survive is a selective, cost-matched win localized by ablation (Section 5). We state plainly which STP components are pre-empted and concede them (Section 2, Section 6).

## 2. Related Work

**Iterative, active, and adaptive RAG.** The reframing of retrieval as a multi-step, uncertainty-aware loop is established. [FLARE [Jiang et al., 2023]](https://arxiv.org/abs/2305.06983) triggers retrieval when upcoming-token confidence is low and forms forward-looking queries from anticipated content. [IRCoT [Trivedi et al., 2023]](https://arxiv.org/abs/2212.10509) interleaves retrieval with each chain-of-thought step so that query *t+1* depends on accumulated reasoning and evidence. [ITER-RETGEN [Shao et al., 2023]](https://arxiv.org/abs/2305.15294) feeds each round's full generation back as the next query. [Self-RAG [Asai et al., 2024]](https://arxiv.org/abs/2310.11511) trains reflection tokens that decide on-demand when to retrieve and critique relevance. [Adaptive-RAG [Jeong et al., 2024]](https://arxiv.org/abs/2403.14403) routes queries across no-/single-/multi-step retrieval by predicted complexity. [DRAGIN [Su et al., 2024]](https://arxiv.org/abs/2403.10081) decides when to retrieve from token-level uncertainty and what to retrieve by reconstructing the query from self-attention over the whole context. [IterDRAG [Yue et al., 2024]](https://arxiv.org/abs/2410.04343) quantifies the compute-versus-coverage tradeoff of iterative sub-query retrieval. STP's claims of sequential trajectories, history-aware state, and uncertainty-driven retrieval are each instantiated by these methods; we do not contest this and position STP as adding only an explicit frontier objective on top of the same loop.

**Agentic and RL-trained search agents.** Retrieval-as-action-in-a-loop is the agentic-search paradigm. [ReAct [Yao et al., 2023]](https://arxiv.org/abs/2210.03629) interleaves reasoning with discrete search/lookup actions, producing a thought–action–observation trajectory; [WebGPT [Nakano et al., 2021]](https://arxiv.org/abs/2112.09332) trained a browser-operating policy with human feedback as early as 2021. The modern RL-trained line — [Search-R1 [Jin et al., 2025]](https://arxiv.org/abs/2503.09516), [R1-Searcher [Song et al., 2025]](https://arxiv.org/abs/2503.05592), [ReSearch [Chen et al., 2025]](https://arxiv.org/abs/2503.19470), and [DeepResearcher [Zheng et al., 2025]](https://arxiv.org/abs/2504.03160) — trains multi-turn retrieval policies end-to-end with outcome rewards, and a [survey of RL-based agentic search [Wu et al., 2025]](https://arxiv.org/abs/2510.16724) already taxonomizes the design space (search intensity, depth, when-to-retrieve, multi-agent). STP's trajectory generation, stateful MDP, RL policy, and policy taxonomy are pre-empted here; our distinction is a reward-shaping term, not a new control structure.

**Tree- and beam-search over retrieval/reasoning paths.** [MCTS-RAG [Hu et al., 2025]](https://arxiv.org/abs/2503.20757) integrates retrieval into Monte Carlo Tree Search with backtracking; [Tree of Thoughts [Yao et al., 2023]](https://arxiv.org/abs/2305.10601) and [RAP [Hao et al., 2023]](https://aclanthology.org/2023.emnlp-main.507/) frame reasoning as value-guided tree search with explicit exploration/exploitation; [LATS [Zhou et al., 2024]](https://arxiv.org/abs/2310.04406) unifies reasoning, acting, and retrieval under MCTS with self-reflection; [PropRAG [Liu et al., 2025]](https://arxiv.org/abs/2504.18070) does LLM-free beam search over proposition paths. We treat these as approximate planners for our non-myopic objective rather than as competitors at the formalism level; STP's "controlled jumps with return" is their backtracking.

**Graph-based and multi-hop KG retrieval.** [Think-on-Graph [Sun et al., 2024]](https://arxiv.org/abs/2307.07697) runs LLM-scored beam search over a knowledge graph; [HippoRAG [Gutiérrez et al., 2024]](https://arxiv.org/abs/2405.14831) uses Personalized PageRank seeded from query entities to reach relationally distant passages; [GraphRAG [Edge et al., 2024]](https://arxiv.org/abs/2404.16130) builds an entity graph with hierarchical community summaries; [PathRAG [Chen et al., 2025]](https://arxiv.org/abs/2502.14902) retrieves and prunes relational *paths*; [LightRAG [Guo et al., 2024]](https://arxiv.org/abs/2410.05779) fuses graph traversal with dual-level embedding retrieval. Most directly, [HopRAG [Liu et al., 2025]](https://arxiv.org/abs/2502.12442) builds LLM-generated pseudo-query edges and hops to the most promising neighbor via a learned "helpfulness" score — a concrete bridge-edge traversal with transition scoring. HopRAG is our nearest competitor; our Bridge Score differs from its helpfulness in being weighted by the *unreached* answer frontier rather than by answer-relevance alone (Section 6).

**RL/MDP retrieval and KG path-walking.** Retrieval as sequential decision-making is old. [Nogueira and Cho [2017]](https://aclanthology.org/D17-1061/) train a query-reformulation policy with REINFORCE optimizing recall, not similarity. [DeepPath [Xiong et al., 2017]](https://aclanthology.org/D17-1060/) walks a KG in continuous embedding space with a reward combining accuracy, *diversity*, and efficiency; [MINERVA [Das et al., 2018]](https://arxiv.org/abs/1711.05851) navigates a KG conditioned on full path history (an RNN-encoded "exploration memory"); [SR [Zhang et al., 2022]](https://arxiv.org/abs/2202.13296) learns a sequential subgraph-expansion policy; [Multi-step Retriever-Reader [Das et al., 2019]](https://arxiv.org/abs/1905.05733) reformulates the dense query vector across steps; [Learning to Retrieve Reasoning Paths [Asai et al., 2020]](https://openreview.net/forum?id=SJgVHkrYDH) retrieves each document conditioned on previously retrieved ones; [RAG-Gym [Xiong et al., 2025]](https://arxiv.org/abs/2502.13957) supervises the multi-step search process with a learned process reward. These collectively pre-empt STP's MDP, exploration memory, continuous-space operation, and RL-policy claims, several by up to eight years.

**Active learning and Bayesian experimental design (EIG).** Using expected information gain rather than a heuristic as the acquisition objective is the textbook active-learning formulation. [BALD [Houlsby et al., 2011]](https://arxiv.org/abs/1112.5745) selects the next query maximizing mutual information about model parameters; [BatchBALD [Kirsch et al., 2019]](https://arxiv.org/abs/1906.08158) extends this to redundancy-aware batch selection; [Deep Adaptive Design [Foster et al., 2021]](https://arxiv.org/abs/2103.02438) amortizes *sequential* EIG into a design-policy network. EIG has been applied directly to retrieval [ITAL [Barz et al., 2018]](https://arxiv.org/abs/1809.02337) and to RAG reranking [InfoGain-RAG [Wang et al., 2025]](https://arxiv.org/abs/2509.12765), as well as to information-seeking question generation [UoT [Hu et al., 2024]](https://arxiv.org/abs/2402.03271); [White et al., 2023](https://arxiv.org/abs/2311.08584); [Mazzaccara et al., 2024](https://arxiv.org/abs/2406.17453). Our EIG objective is *not* novel; we adopt it as established and use it only as the scaffolding within which the Bridge Score is defined. Answer-uncertainty estimation follows [semantic entropy [Kuhn et al., 2023]](https://openreview.net/forum?id=VD-AYtP0dve).

**Network science of bridges and brokerage.** The Bridge Score's conceptual core is decades old. Betweenness centrality formalizes a node's control of flow between separated regions [Freeman, 1977](https://www.jstor.org/stable/3033543); the strength-of-weak-ties thesis identifies bridges as conduits of novel information [Granovetter, 1973](https://www.jstor.org/stable/2776392); structural-hole theory ties brokerage to non-redundant information benefit [Burt, 1992](https://www.hup.harvard.edu/books/9780674843714); brokerage roles are formally typed [Gould and Fernandez, 1989](https://www.jstor.org/stable/270949); edge betweenness identifies inter-community bridges [Girvan and Newman, 2002](https://www.pnas.org/doi/10.1073/pnas.122653799); and **bridging centrality** defines a per-node bridging coefficient as the probability of leaving the local neighborhood [Hwang et al., 2008](https://dl.acm.org/doi/10.1145/1401890.1401934). Literature-based discovery chains bridging concepts across disjoint literatures [Swanson, 1986](https://muse.jhu.edu/article/403510/summary). We treat Hwang's bridging coefficient as the most direct antecedent and state our generalization against it explicitly (Section 4.5).

**Intrinsic motivation / curiosity in RL.** Frontier-seeking exploration via information gain is the curiosity literature: [VIME [Houthooft et al., 2016]](https://arxiv.org/abs/1605.09674), [count-based pseudo-counts [Bellemare et al., 2016]](https://papers.nips.cc/paper/6383-unifying-count-based-exploration-and-intrinsic-motivation), and [ICM [Pathak et al., 2017]](https://pathak22.github.io/noreward-rl/resources/icml17.pdf). Most relevant, [Patankar et al., 2023](https://arxiv.org/abs/2307.04962) train a GNN-RL agent with topological-curiosity rewards that score moves opening access to novel graph regions, beating PageRank. Our learnable Bridge Score reuses this machinery; its only distinction is a *query-conditioned, answer-frontier* reward rather than query-free topological surprise.

**Embedding-space geometry and the substrate question.** The viability of "continuous semantic navigation" is contradicted by primary results: token embeddings provably violate the manifold hypothesis, with unstable geodesics [Robinson et al., 2025](https://arxiv.org/abs/2504.01002); linear interpolation diverges from geodesics on curved latent spaces [Shao et al., 2018](https://openaccess.thecvf.com/content_cvpr_2018_workshops/papers/w10/Shao_The_Riemannian_Geometry_CVPR_2018_paper.pdf); and synthetic intermediate points are usable only as transient queries that resolve to corpus nodes [HyDE [Gao et al., 2023]](https://arxiv.org/abs/2212.10496). Production ANN is itself a multi-step coarse-to-fine graph traversal [HNSW [Malkov and Yashunin, 2018]](https://arxiv.org/abs/1603.09320), and even "manifold-aware" retrieval computes geodesics over a discrete k-NN graph [MA-DPR [Liu et al., 2025]](https://arxiv.org/abs/2509.13562); dense iterative path search predates STP [BeamDR [Zhao et al., 2021]](https://arxiv.org/abs/2104.05883). We accept these results and commit to a discrete substrate (Section 3).

**Multi-hop benchmarks.** Our evaluation rests on existing benchmarks built to require chained, distant facts: [HotpotQA [Yang et al., 2018]](https://arxiv.org/abs/1809.09600), [2WikiMultiHopQA [Ho et al., 2020]](https://aclanthology.org/2020.coling-main.580/), [MuSiQue [Trivedi et al., 2022]](https://arxiv.org/abs/2108.00573), [Bamboogle / Self-Ask [Press et al., 2023]](https://arxiv.org/abs/2210.03350), [StrategyQA [Geva et al., 2021]](https://arxiv.org/abs/2101.02235), [FanOutQA [Zhu et al., 2024]](https://arxiv.org/abs/2402.14116), and [FRAMES [Krishna et al., 2025]](https://arxiv.org/abs/2409.12941). These provide per-hop evidence-path supervision (used to train and evaluate the Bridge Score) and the bridge/non-bridge split that makes our hypothesis falsifiable.

## 3. Substrate: the discrete corpus graph

We commit to a single substrate and justify the choice from primary geometric results, because every downstream construct — trajectory, information gain, Bridge Score — is defined over it.

Let the corpus be chunks $\{c_i\}$ with frozen embeddings $x_i \in \mathbb{R}^d$. We build a symmetric $k$-NN similarity graph $G=(V,E,w)$ with $w_{ij}=\max(0,\cos(x_i,x_j))$, optionally augmented with relational (KG) or LLM-generated pseudo-query edges. The only retrievable states are corpus nodes. A continuous vector $v\in\mathbb{R}^d$ is admitted **only** as a transient query waypoint, immediately resolved to its neighborhood $\mathrm{NN}_k(v)$; it is never itself a returnable item.

This is not a convenience. Token-embedding subspaces are provably not manifolds and their geodesics are unstable and need not track semantic distance [Robinson et al., 2025](https://arxiv.org/abs/2504.01002); naive interpolation drifts off-manifold absent a pullback metric [Shao et al., 2018](https://openaccess.thecvf.com/content_cvpr_2018_workshops/papers/w10/Shao_The_Riemannian_Geometry_CVPR_2018_paper.pdf); synthetic intermediate points function only as queries that snap back to nodes [HyDE [Gao et al., 2023]](https://arxiv.org/abs/2212.10496); and even manifold-aware retrieval keeps a discrete graph substrate [MA-DPR [Liu et al., 2025]](https://arxiv.org/abs/2509.13562). We therefore make no claim of free continuous navigation. "Continuous operation" in STP means *continuously move the query waypoint, discretely resolve every step to corpus nodes* — which collapses to the discrete belief-MDP at each step. We treat this as a deliberate scoping decision that removes STP's most refutable claim rather than defending it.

## 4. Method

### 4.1 The belief-MDP

STP is a finite-horizon **POMDP** whose hidden variable is the latent answer $a^\star$, equivalently a **belief-MDP** over $b_t = p(a^\star \mid h_t)$, where $h_t$ is the interaction history. The sufficient statistic is the history $h_t = (q, (P_0,o_0),\dots,(P_{t-1},o_{t-1}))$, of which the "exploration memory" $\psi(h_t)=(V_t, F_t, c_t)$ — visited embeddings, failed/low-yield paths, coverage pseudo-counts — is a deterministic feature map. The "semantic location" is the last resolved node. These are not competing definitions of state; they are cross-sections of one belief-MDP, and the POMDP-over-$a^\star$ / belief-MDP-over-$b_t$ / history-MDP-over-$h_t$ equivalence is standard [Åström, 1965; Kaelbling et al., 1998](https://www.sciencedirect.com/science/article/pii/S000437029800023X). STP introduces none of this structure; it selects the EIG objective and the feature map $\psi$.

Formally $\mathcal{M}=\langle \mathcal{S},\mathcal{A},\Omega,T,O,R,\gamma,H,b_0\rangle$ with hidden answer $a^\star$ and observed retrieved set $R_t$, so $\mathcal{S}=\mathcal{A}_{\text{ans}}\times 2^{\mathcal{C}}$.

### 4.2 Unified action

A single action object unifies *when*, *what/where*, and *stop*:
$$
\mathcal{A} = \{\texttt{STOP}\} \cup \{\texttt{RETRIEVE}(u) : u\in\mathcal{U}\},
$$
where $u$ is either a graph neighbor of the current node (discrete move) or a waypoint $w\in\mathbb{R}^d$ resolved by $\mathrm{NN}_k(w)$. The displacement $\lVert w - x_{s_t}\rVert$ encodes "how far"; a large-displacement action is a *controlled semantic jump*, and a "return" is an action whose $u$ points into the previously visited set $V_t$. We note plainly that this unification — one policy over when/what/where/stop — is *integrative packaging*: each component is individually pre-empted ([DRAGIN](https://arxiv.org/abs/2403.10081) = trigger, [HopRAG](https://arxiv.org/abs/2502.12442) = direction, [Stop-RAG](https://arxiv.org/abs/2510.14337) = stop).

### 4.3 Observations and belief update

On $\texttt{RETRIEVE}(u)$ the environment returns $o_t=\mathrm{NN}_k(u)$ and contents; given the fixed corpus the observation is deterministic in $u$. With a reader-induced likelihood $p(o_t\mid a^\star)$, the belief transition is Bayes:
$$
b_{t+1}(a)=\frac{p(o_t\mid a)\,b_t(a)}{\sum_{a'}p(o_t\mid a')\,b_t(a')}.
$$
Exploration memory updates deterministically: $V_{t+1}=V_t\cup\{x_d : d\in o_t\}$, with pseudo-counts and failed-path set updated analogously.

### 4.4 Expected information gain about the answer

Let $\mathbb{H}[b]=-\sum_a b(a)\log b(a)$. The acquisition value of a candidate action is the mutual information between the answer and the prospective observation:
$$
\mathrm{EIG}(u\mid h_t) = I(a^\star;\,o\mid h_t,u) = \mathbb{H}[b_t] - \mathbb{E}_{o\sim p(\cdot\mid h_t,u)}\big[\mathbb{H}[\mathcal{B}(b_t,u,o)]\big].
$$
This is the BALD acquisition function [Houlsby et al., 2011](https://arxiv.org/abs/1112.5745) with the answer $a^\star$ substituted for model parameters $\theta$, and its use as a retrieval objective is established [ITAL, 2018](https://arxiv.org/abs/1809.02337); [InfoGain-RAG, 2025](https://arxiv.org/abs/2509.12765). With observations on a discrete shortlist, the realized gain $\widehat{\mathrm{EIG}}(u)=\mathbb{H}[b_t]-\mathbb{H}[b_{t+1}]$ is the InfoGain-RAG "document information gain" form. We do not claim novelty for EIG-as-objective. The trajectory objective telescopes to total uncertainty reduction $\mathbb{H}[b_0]-\mathbb{H}[b_H]$ under a budget, which is sequential Bayesian experimental design [DAD, 2021](https://arxiv.org/abs/2103.02438) and yields a value-of-information stopping rule (stop when the best candidate's marginal EIG falls below the retrieval cost), as in [Stop-RAG, 2025](https://arxiv.org/abs/2510.14337).

For free-text answers we estimate $\mathbb{H}[b_t]$ via **semantic entropy** [Kuhn et al., 2023](https://openreview.net/forum?id=VD-AYtP0dve): sample $S$ answers, cluster by bidirectional entailment, and take entropy over cluster probabilities; for closed-set QA we use normalized answer-token probabilities directly. EIG over a *set* uses greedy conditional gain to avoid redundancy [BatchBALD, 2019](https://arxiv.org/abs/1906.08158).

### 4.5 The Bridge Score (the contribution)

Define the **answer-frontier potential** $\rho_t:V\to[0,1]$,
$$
\rho_t(v) = \underbrace{r(v\mid q)}_{\text{relevance-to-goal}}\cdot\underbrace{\big(1-\mathrm{cov}_t(v)\big)}_{\text{not-yet-explored}},\qquad \mathrm{cov}_t(v)=\max_{u\in V_t}w_{uv}.
$$
The Bridge Score of a candidate node $v\notin V_t$ is a **conditional, frontier-weighted, flow betweenness across the explored cut**. With $\sigma(a,z)$ the number of shortest paths $a\rightsquigarrow z$ and $\sigma(a,z\mid v)$ those through $v$:
$$
\boxed{\;\mathrm{BS}_t(v)=\sum_{a\in V_t}\sum_{z\in V\setminus V_t}\rho_t(z)\,\pi_t(a)\,\frac{\sigma(a,z\mid v)}{\sigma(a,z)}\;}
$$
where $\pi_t$ weights explored anchors by recency/relevance.

This is exactly Freeman betweenness restricted to source-set $V_t$ and frontier-weighted sink-set. The reductions make the relationship to prior art precise and falsifiable:

- Setting $V_t=V$, sink-set $=V$, $\rho_t\equiv1$, $\pi_t\equiv1$ recovers **Freeman betweenness** [1977](https://www.jstor.org/stable/3033543) exactly. Freeman is the unconditional, query-free, history-free special case.
- Using the through-edge ratio yields the demand-weighted analogue of **edge betweenness** [Girvan and Newman, 2002](https://www.pnas.org/doi/10.1073/pnas.122653799).
- Computing tie redundancy over the explored neighborhood recovers a frontier-weighted **structural-hole / brokerage** quantity [Burt, 1992](https://www.hup.harvard.edu/books/9780674843714).
- **Critically, it is not Hwang's bridging coefficient** [2008](https://dl.acm.org/doi/10.1145/1401890.1401934): that quantity is query-free, history-free, and computed on the static topology. $\mathrm{BS}_t$ is conditioned on $V_t$ (history-aware) and weighted by $\rho_t$ (query- and frontier-aware). Hwang scores how bridge-y a node is in the fixed graph; $\mathrm{BS}_t$ scores how much a node bridges *the agent's current memory* to *the answer's currently-unreachable region*.

### 4.6 Tractable surrogate and learnable form

Exact $\mathrm{BS}_t$ is $O(|V|\cdot|E|)$. We replace shortest-path flow with a diffusion surrogate. Let $P=D^{-1}W$ and seed Personalized PageRank from the *explored* set $V_t$ (whereas [HippoRAG, 2024](https://arxiv.org/abs/2405.14831) seeds from query entities — the history-aware twist). In pure embedding terms with kernel $\kappa(x,y)=e^{-\lVert x-y\rVert^2/2\tau}$:
$$
\widehat{\mathrm{BS}}_t(v)\;\propto\;\underbrace{\Big(1-\max_{u\in V_t}\kappa(x_v,x_u)\Big)}_{\text{novelty vs.\ memory}}\cdot\underbrace{\mathbb{E}_{z\sim\rho_t}\!\big[\kappa(x_v,x_z)\big]}_{\text{frontier proximity}}\cdot\underbrace{\mathbb{H}\big(\kappa(x_v,\cdot)\big)}_{\text{connective breadth}}.
$$
All three factors are load-bearing: dropping novelty recovers a relevance reranker; dropping frontier proximity recovers a query-free curiosity bonus; dropping breadth recovers an MMR-style diversity term. The product, conditioned on $V_t$ and weighted by $\rho_t$, is the object with no verbatim antecedent.

The learnable head $\mathrm{BS}_\theta(v\mid s_t)=\phi_\theta(x_v,\mathrm{pool}(V_t),q)$ is trained on a **frontier-expansion target** — not answer-correctness:
$$
y_t(v)=\big|\mathcal{R}(V_t\cup\{v\})\cap A\big| - \big|\mathcal{R}(V_t)\cap A\big|,
$$
where $A$ is the gold supporting-fact set (annotated by [2WikiMultiHopQA](https://aclanthology.org/2020.coling-main.580/), [MuSiQue](https://arxiv.org/abs/2108.00573)) and $\mathcal{R}(\cdot)$ is the $\tau$-hop reachable set. A node that is itself not gold evidence but unlocks previously-unreachable gold evidence gets high $y_t$ here and near-zero under answer-relevance training — the distinction from [HopRAG's helpfulness [2025]](https://arxiv.org/abs/2502.12442) and from outcome value functions [Stop-RAG, 2025](https://arxiv.org/abs/2510.14337). The machinery (GNN + intrinsic graph reward) is itself pre-empted [Patankar et al., 2023](https://arxiv.org/abs/2307.04962); the novelty is solely that the reward is query-conditioned reachable-*answer*-frontier gain over the explored set, not query-free topological surprise. We state this as a specialization, not a paradigm.

### 4.7 Reward and objective

$$
R(h_t,u_t,o_t)=\underbrace{\mathrm{IG}(u_t,o_t)}_{\text{answer-directed}}+\underbrace{\alpha\,\mathrm{BS}_t(u_t)}_{\text{frontier}}-\underbrace{\lambda\,\mathrm{cost}(u_t)}_{\text{budget}},
$$
with terminal answer utility at $\texttt{STOP}$. The policy maximizes $J(\pi)=\mathbb{E}_\pi[\sum_t\gamma^t R + \gamma^H U(a_{\texttt{STOP}})]$. The policy "taxonomy" (conservative, exploratory, bridge-discovery, coarse-to-fine, RL, multi-agent) is recovered as regions of the parameter space $(\lambda,\alpha,\text{action-set structure})$ and carries no independent formal content; we do not present it as a contribution.

## 5. Experiments (design)

The experiment tests the one empirically open claim: whether frontier-weighted information-gain trajectory retrieval beats strong iterative/agentic RAG *specifically on bridge-jump tasks, at matched compute*. If STP wins only by spending more retrieval, it demonstrates nothing beyond inference scaling [IterDRAG, 2024](https://arxiv.org/abs/2410.04343). Per-cost evaluation is therefore the hypothesis, not a courtesy.

**Hypotheses (pre-registered).** (H1) On bridge-jump items at a fixed token budget, full STP beats the best baseline including HopRAG by $\geq 3$ answer-F1 at equal-or-lower cost (one-sided paired bootstrap, $\alpha=0.05$). (H2) STP's recall of the specific across-low-similarity gold passage exceeds every baseline, with the gap larger on bridge hops than on non-bridge hops. (H3, specificity) On non-bridge multi-hop items, STP does *not* beat iterative-RAG by more than 1 F1 at matched cost — the effect must be selective.

**Bridge-jump labeling (frozen, published before any run).** For each gold chain, walk it hop-by-hop; at hop $i$, retrieve top-K from the accumulated context $C_{<i}$ and check whether gold passage $i$ is present for $K\in\{5,10,20\}$. A *bridge hop* is a gold hop absent from top-K; a *bridge-jump item* has $\geq 1$ bridge hop. H1/H2 live on bridge items; H3 on non-bridge items.

**Datasets.** Primary: [MuSiQue](https://arxiv.org/abs/2108.00573) (adversarially filtered against shortcutting), [2WikiMultiHopQA](https://aclanthology.org/2020.coling-main.580/) (per-hop evidence-path supervision), [FRAMES](https://arxiv.org/abs/2409.12941) (2–15 articles, end-to-end). Stress: [Bamboogle](https://arxiv.org/abs/2210.03350). Non-bridge control: [HotpotQA distractor](https://arxiv.org/abs/1809.09600), used *only* for H3 because it is partly shortcuttable.

**Baselines (shared encoder, corpus, reader, budget).** Single-shot top-K and a cost-matched top-K ceiling; [IRCoT](https://arxiv.org/abs/2212.10509); [FLARE](https://arxiv.org/abs/2305.06983); [Self-Ask](https://arxiv.org/abs/2210.03350); [Adaptive-RAG](https://arxiv.org/abs/2403.14403); [GraphRAG](https://arxiv.org/abs/2404.16130); **[HopRAG](https://arxiv.org/abs/2502.12442) — the real adversary**; [IterDRAG](https://arxiv.org/abs/2410.04343). The critical control baseline is STP with the Bridge Score removed but EIG retained: if it matches full STP on bridge items, the contribution is empirically inert.

**Metrics.** Answer EM/F1 reported *separately* on bridge vs non-bridge subsets (never pooled); hop-resolved recall and bridge-hop recall; supporting-fact/evidence-path F1; and a fairness axis of retrieval calls, LLM forward passes, prompt+generated tokens, and latency. **Cost rule (pre-registered):** every LLM call used to estimate a candidate's EIG counts against STP's budget at full price; an amortized embedding-only EIG surrogate's inference counts as retrieval, its training cost reported once. There is no free EIG. The headline result is the F1-versus-token-budget Pareto frontier on bridge items.

**Ablations.** A $2\times2\times2$ over EIG ranking, Bridge Score, and exploration memory, plus two Bridge-Score-internal ablations: *BS-relevance-only* (drop the frontier factor; equals HopRAG-style scoring) and *BS-novelty-only* (drop query conditioning; equals a curiosity bonus). Confirmation requires that full $\beta$ beat both.

**Falsification.** The claim fails if (i) the bridge win disappears once EIG inner-loop calls are charged; (ii) STP $\approx$ or $<$ HopRAG on bridge items at matched cost; (iii) the EIG+memory ablation matches full STP; or (iv) STP wins uniformly on bridge and non-bridge alike (the advantage is generic iterative compute, not bridges).

**Pilot (gate before LLM spend).** A NumPy/NetworkX simulator over a planted-bridge stochastic block model with node2vec embeddings, oracle EIG (analytic belief pruning over candidate answer nodes), and oracle Bridge Score. Sweeping the number of required bridge crossings and cross-edge weight, the predicted signature is that cosine-greedy collapses, EIG-greedy partially degrades (no drive to *leave* an exhausted cluster), and STP retains success — with the gap present only when bridges exist (synthetic H3). If oracle-EIG-greedy matches oracle-STP even at three crossings, the mechanism is falsified in silico and the program stops before any LLM cost. The pilot also calibrates the bridge-difficulty regime the real benchmark should over-sample.

## 6. Limitations

We are explicit about what does not survive scrutiny.

**Most STP mechanisms are pre-empted, and we concede them.** Sequential trajectory generation, history-aware state, EIG/uncertainty-driven retrieval, controlled jumps with return, the retrieval-as-MDP formulation, the policy taxonomy, and continuous-space operation are each instantiated by named prior work, several predating this paper by up to eight years (Section 2). The spatial vocabulary — "trajectory," "exploration memory," "semantic jump," "bridge" — is in large part a re-labeling of iterative/agentic RAG and RL graph-walking. None of these is claimed as novel.

**The contribution is narrow and incremental.** It is one functional: a state-conditioned, answer-frontier-weighted betweenness with a diffusion surrogate and a reachable-gold-evidence training target. Its machinery (betweenness, PPR, GNN intrinsic rewards, EIG) is entirely borrowed; its only distinguishing feature is the joint conditioning on $(q, V_t, A)$ that simultaneously makes it query-conditioned (unlike curiosity bonuses) and frontier-seeking (unlike HopRAG's answer-relevance edge scoring). This is a specialization, and it may prove empirically inert — Section 5 is designed so that the EIG-only and BS-relevance-only ablations can falsify it.

**The continuous-navigation claim is dropped, not solved.** Token embeddings are provably not a manifold [Robinson et al., 2025](https://arxiv.org/abs/2504.01002); we therefore restrict to a discrete substrate and treat continuous waypoints as transient queries. STP says nothing about navigating a smooth semantic space, and any reading of it as doing so is unsupported by current geometry.

**The EIG objective is expensive and the cost accounting is adversarial to STP by design.** Per-candidate semantic-entropy estimation requires LLM forward passes; without the amortized surrogate, STP may simply be priced out of the per-cost frontier it must dominate. The honest outcome may be that EIG-as-ranking is not competitive at retrieval latency.

**Benchmark and supervision limits.** Bridge labeling depends on the chosen encoder and K; the gold-evidence reachability target depends on annotation completeness, which is imperfect on MuSiQue and FRAMES. Yes/no datasets (StrategyQA) give weak F1 signal. We have not established that *deliberate detours* are credited rather than penalized by existing benchmarks, which generally treat off-path retrieval as distractor noise; a benchmark that credits productive bridges remains future work.

**Net position.** STP should be read as an integrative synthesis of established iterative/agentic-RAG and RL graph-exploration machinery for the multi-hop RAG setting, plus one incremental functional — the frontier-weighted Bridge Score — whose value is an empirical question this paper poses but does not yet answer. It is not a new retrieval paradigm, and we do not present it as one.
